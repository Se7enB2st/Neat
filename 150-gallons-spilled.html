Fire chief: 150 gallons spilled in Center Township gas station incident

CENTER TWP. -- Emergency responders strongly encouraged evacuations Saturday night for a neighborhood, plaza and two hotels after a fuel leak from a tanker truck, officials said.

The incident spilled 150 gallons across a roadway and down a sewer drain, Center Township Fire Chief Bill Brucker said. Multiple Wilhelm Drive residents said that caused a strong smell of gasoline there Sunday.

Firefighters had responded to the scene at about 7:45 p.m. Saturday, and they eventually conducted door-to-door stops encouraging people to evacuate. Emergency responders worked at the scene for about 7.5 hours, Center Township Fire Chief Bill Brucker said.

The leak came after the tanker truck had gone to the wrong gas station and sheared off a valve while turning around there, Brucker said. The vehicle then arrived at a Shell gas station, its correct destination.

Brucker said 2,350 gallons of fuel from the 2,500-gallon tank on the truck were transferred to a tank car.

Emergency responders placed a foam solution over the area of the fuel spill as well as oil-dry product, an absorbent substance like cat litter commonly used at motor vehicle accident scenes.

Response efforts also included the Beaver County Hazardous Materials Response Team, service company Weavertown Environmental Group, Center Township police, and additional firefighters from Monaca and Potter Township.

To help clear the fuel from a creek behind several Wilhelm Drive homes, crews placed booms, similar to swim noodles, Saturday night. As of Sunday, other sheet-like absorbents scoured part of the creek.

A worker from the state Department of Environmental Protection visited the creek Sunday, residents said.

On Saturday night, officials originally sent people to the Monaca Turners, residents said. They were ultimately sent to a shelter at the Brodhead Road fire station, and a representative for the fire department said 24 people, mainly residents, were there at one point.

Many people spent the night with family members nearby, but some also rented hotel rooms, where they also took their dogs.

Officials also recommended evacuations for The Inn and Comfort Suites, near the Shell gas station, as well as Center Square plaza.

A hotel patron, Juan Rolando, said he was instructed to evacuate to Penn State-Beaver. Rolando said he spent several hours with others in a Walmart parking lot, checking back to see when the scene was clear.
